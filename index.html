Miyamoto Musashi

Greatest Samurai Swordsman of Japan's Edo period.

  • 1 March, 1584 — Miyamoto Musashi was born in Harima Province, Japan, to Shinmen Munisai, a martial artist and master of the sword and jutte.
  • 1591 — When Musashi turned seven, he was raised by his uncle Dorinbo, in Shoreian temple. Learned about Buddhism and was taught the basic reading and writing skills.
  • 1597 — He won his first ever duel, at the age of thirteen, against the samurai Arima Kihei.
  • 1599 — He moved out of his village and left his family possessions with his sister and her husband. He went on spending much of his time engaging in duels.
  • 1600 — War broke out between the Toyotomi and the Tokugawa during which he supported and fought on the Toyotomi side which lost in the Battle of Sekigahara. Afterwhich he escaped bounty hunters and became one of the rōnin (masterless samurai).
  • 1604 — At 21 years of age, Musashi's fame is spread all over Japan after he won three important duels agains three members of the Yoshioka Family, who were previously responsible for the Shogun's "official Swordsman School".
    1. First, Musashi challenged the master of the Yoshioka School, Seijuro Kenpo, he won the duel and received the title of 'Unrivaled Under Heaven'.
    2. The second, was the last of the "Kenpo Brothers" after Seijuro died, Denshichiro.
    3. The third was Seijuro's son, Matashichiro, but he planned with the rest of all the students and followers of Yoshioka School to seek revenge on Musashi. Musashi fought and defeated 60 opponents at the same time, all of them armed with swords, spears, bows, and arrows and even muskets. This was the end of Yoshioka House and the start of the Miyamoto Musashi's legend.
  • From 1605 to 1612 — He travelled extensively all over Japan for his Musho Shugyo, a kind of warrior's pilgrimage. It was during this time that he honed his skills for the duels. During this period, the following are duels
    • Hozoin Temple Warrior Monks, famous for their Sojutsu (or Yarijutsu) School (spear techniques);
    • Muso Gonnosuke, the founder of the Jojutsu (Staff techniques) Shindo Muso Ryu, a technique also trained in the Niten Institut. Gonnosuke created the Jojutsu after being defeated by Musashi Sensei as a way to defeat him. There is a long tradition recalling about a possible second duel against Musashi Sensei, where Gonnosuke had reached a tied combat, with no winner declared;
    • Shishido Baiken, a kusarigama (a chain-sickle weapon) specialist. The kusarigama (a sickle with a metal chain with a heavy iron weight at the end) is a very exotic weapon and very hard to handle as well, which is also trained in some styles at the Niten Institut.
  • 1607 — Munisai (Musashi's father) passes his teachins onto Musashi.
  • 1608 — Duels Musō Gonnosuke, master of the five-foot staff in Edo.
  • 1611 — Started practicing zazen at the Myoshinji Temple.
  • 13 April, 1612 — Had a duel with Sasaki Kojirō, at Funajima Island. Musashi

  • 1641 — Writes Hyoho Sanju-go.

  • 1643 — Migrates into Reigandō where he lives as a hermit.
  • 19 May, 1645 — Finishes Go Rin No Sho/The Book of Five Rings. Dies from what is believed to be lung cancer.
